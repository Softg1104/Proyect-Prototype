Sustainable Consumption

Our mission

The goal is promoting resource efficiency, reducing waste, and encouraging sustainable practices across all levels of production and consumption. As well as acknowledging the social inequalities that lie at the core of this problem.

The problem we are trying to solve

Nowadays everything is standardized to reduce costs and programmed to be obsolete. The biggest social problems that come with this problem are labor exploitation and economic inequality. While companies profit, many workers and small-scale producers struggle to meet basic needs this way exacerbating global inequalities and limiting access to resources and opportunities for marginalized groups.

Raise awareness

We aim to raise awareness and inspire more thoughtful consumption behaviors. It's only by questioning and redefining our consumption habits and patterns that we can hope to build and go towards a more sustainable future for all.We think that our generation finds ourselves in the middle of this problem, we were born already in a consumerist society but also we are paying the environmental consequences of this unethical lifestyle. The system we are living creates new desires and needs

What can we do as consumers

  1. Choose sustainable products

    Opt for goods made from environmentally friendly materials, such as organic, recycled, or biodegradable options. Look for certifications like Fair Trade, which ensure that products are ethically produced.

  2. Support local and ethical Brands:

    Prioritize buying from businesses that are transparent about their supply chains and who treat their workers fairly. By supporting local brands, you can reduce your carbon footprint and help boost your local economy.

  3. Buy less, buy better:

    Instead of focusing on quantity, invest in quality products that are durable and designed to last. This reduces waste and ensures that the items you buy are worth the investment.

  4. Educate yourself and others

    Share knowledge about the importance of conscious consumption with your community. Encourage others to think critically about the impact of their purchasing decisions and how they can contribute to positive change.

  5. Mindfulness on resource consumption

    In addition to thoughtful purchasing, being conscious of how we use energy, water, and other resources in our daily lives is crucial. Simple steps like turning off unused lights or conserving water can have a significant impact.
